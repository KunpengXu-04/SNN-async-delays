# Results: simultaneous temporal checkpoint mechanism audit v1

Date: 2026-07-13  
Source: final checkpoints from `simultaneous_temporal_viability_preflight_v1`  
Scope: exhaustive 64-pattern, read-only post-hoc mechanism audit

## Audit questions

1. Does hidden silence inside an output window imply that no delayed event
   reaches the output in that window?
2. Does each window retain gradients to output weights and WAD delays?
3. Are the learned delays geometrically incapable of late arrival, or are
   candidate/realized late paths present but weakly discriminative?
4. Is the training objective aligned with worst-window balanced evaluation?

No checkpoint was trained, selected or modified by this audit.

## Central correction to preflight v1

Preflight v1 formally failed its preregistered WAD hidden-emission gate because
window-3 hidden activity was zero. That proxy is not equivalent to output event
support. Hidden spikes emitted before window 3 can traverse delayed `syn_ho`
and arrive during window 3.

On the exhaustive truth table, WAD window 3 has:

- hidden emission support: `0.000`;
- output synaptic-current support: `.703` of trials;
- output spike activity: `.344` of trials;
- normalized realized hidden-to-output arrival mass: `.117`;
- mean absolute output current: `4.408`.

Therefore the formal v1 failure remains historically valid, but the statement
"WAD has no third-window event support" is false at the output interface. V1
used a mislocated diagnostic gate.

## Arrival geometry

WAD learned delays remain short-biased: input-hidden median `3.43`, hidden-
output median `3.90`. Nevertheless, the two-layer system has late paths. The
normalized candidate two-hop mass in time regions `<10/[10,20)/[20,30)/[30,40)`
is `.166/.477/.311/.046`; the corresponding mass generated by actually
observed hidden spikes is `.173/.467/.242/.117`.

Full-support fixed delay distributes realized mass across all three output
windows (`.219/.320/.378`) and scaffold deliberately concentrates late mass
(`.166/.161/.673`). WAD is late-poor relative to these controls, but not late-
absent.

## Window-specific credit assignment

For WAD's actual post-warmup combined objective, delay-gradient norms by window
are:

| Window | Input-hidden | Hidden-output | Output-weight |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 XOR | .379 | .459 | .135 |
| 1 NAND | .599 | .312 | .175 |
| 2 NOR | 1.195 | .293 | .187 |

The third window has the largest input-hidden delay-gradient norm, so its
failure is not caused by absent gradient. A first-order gradient-descent
direction audit predicts mean effective-delay shifts of `-.061` (input-hidden)
and `-.019` (hidden-output) for the third-window loss: its aggregate local
direction is toward shorter, not longer, delays. This is only a local gradient
diagnostic—not an Adam training trajectory—but it contradicts the assumption
that the third-window loss necessarily rewards later delays.

## Support is not discriminability

WAD signed output-current class gaps are `11.20`, `20.14`, and only `2.31` in
the three windows. Thus window 3 receives events and gradients but carries weak
class separation and remains at balanced accuracy `.5`.

The original objective pooled ordinary BCE across windows. Under uniform input
bits, positive prevalence is `.50` for XOR, `.75` for NAND and `.25` for NOR.
That objective is misaligned with worst-window balanced accuracy and permits a
default-majority NOR solution. This explains why exact-trial/pooled values can
look respectable while NOR balanced accuracy remains at chance.

## Decision

1. Keep the v1 formal fail in the record, but retract its interpretation as
   proof of no output arrival support.
2. Replace hidden-emission gates with delayed output-current and realized-
   arrival gates.
3. Use a window/class-balanced loss for all conditions, not as a WAD-only
   rescue, because the task's operator prevalences differ.
4. Use held-out seeds `{1,42}` for preflight v2; seed 0 has informed this
   redesign and cannot validate it.
5. Do not change WAD initialization, threshold, `dmax`, learning rate or
   optimization schedule in v2. This isolates gate/objective correction from a
   new delay method.
